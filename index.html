Episode 1

Part 1: “The Checklist”

The lab was quiet at this hour, the way Harper liked it. Golden light slanted through the tall windows, catching on the edges of glass beakers and the whiteboards still covered in yesterday’s marker notes.

*Better. That’s better. Can’t stand in front of two hundred people with a sloppy transition between the biomarker panels and the validation data.*

She set the pen down and leaned back in her chair. The white button-up blouse clung softly to the curve of her waist and the full swell of her chest before she absently smoothed it back into place.

Part 1: “The Checklist” (continued)

A soft knock on the open doorframe pulled her attention up.

“Hey, Harper.” It was Daniel from the proteomics team, holding two paper coffee cups.

*They’re always sweet. And I always say no. It’s not that I don’t like them. It’s just… easier this way.*

She saved the file and headed for the elevator. Outside, the golden light of sunset painted the street. She stopped at her usual café and sat by the window with her oat milk latte.

Her phone buzzed with a text from her mom: “Safe travels tomorrow, my serious little genius. We love you. Try to smile at someone cute on the plane.”

Part 2: “The Chaos at Home”

Harper’s key turned in the lock with a familiar click. The smell of garlic and lemon chicken hit her immediately. Cooper came barreling down the hallway.

“Harper’s home!” her mom called from the kitchen.

The kitchen was warm and lived-in. Haley, Matt, and their dad were already there. The table was set for five.

*They’re right. I know they’re right. I am the serious one. The responsible one.*

Halfway through dinner, Haley grinned across the table. “So… you ready to have an actual life in Tokyo or are you going to spend the whole time in your hotel room like a little work goblin?”

Part 3: “The Brief”

The camera found him from a distance. Kane Hale moved through the pulsing nightlife district with quiet certainty.

He stepped inside the high-end strip club without hesitation. The interior hit like a fever dream — dim red and purple lights, bass thumping through the floor.

He cut straight through the crowd until he slipped through a staff-only door into the kitchen, then into the secure room at the back.

Four men stood around a long black table. Kane stopped at the head.

“Contract 47-Alpha. We go in quiet. We take the regional commander alive. No screams. No bodies left identifiable.”

*Another night. Another set of numbers. Another body count that would stay sealed in this world and never touch the one he was trying to keep separate.*
